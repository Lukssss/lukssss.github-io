From its dramatic landscapes to its vibrant ethnic communities, Ha Giang promises an unforgettable experience off the beaten path for those willing to explore its rugged beauty.

Ho Chi Minh City

Southern Vietnam

Vietnam's vibrant southern hub. It boasts a captivating mix of French colonial architecture, bustling markets like Ben Thanh, and iconic landmarks such as Notre-Dame Cathedral. Visitors can explore its rich history at the War Remnants Museum or indulge in diverse cuisine from street food stalls to upscale restaurants. Cat Ba Archipelago

Northern Vietnam

Located in Hai Phong Province, Cat Ba is known as the 3rd biosphere reserve in Vietnam by covering more than 26,000 hectares of high biodiversity. Notably, in 2004, this island was recognized by UNESCO as a Biosphere Reserve Zone. Cat Ba terrain is famous for its karst topography. This kind of topography, characterized by limestone mountains, stunning caves and smooth white sandy beaches, contributes to the unique beauty of this island.

Fansipan

Northern Vietnam

Fansipan, Vietnam's highest peak, is a premier trekking destination in Southeast Asia. Located near Sapa in Lao Cai Province, it offers breathtaking views and a challenging trek through lush forests and rugged terrain. The summit rewards trekkers with unparalleled vistas of mountain ranges and terraced fields. For those seeking a less strenuous journey, a cable car provides an alternative route to experience Fansipan's majestic beauty.

Trang An

Northern Vietnam

Trang An, located in Ninh Binh Province of northern Vietnam, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its stunning karst landscape and rich cultural heritage. This picturesque area features a network of limestone karst formations, lush greenery, and winding waterways, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventurers alike. Visitors to Trang An can embark on scenic boat tours that meander through caves, past rice paddies, and alongside towering limestone cliffs.
